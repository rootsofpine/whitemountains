The Morning My Grandfather Showed Me

December 2023 North Conway
I was seven when he took me up before dawn. We didn't talk much, that wasn't his way. But when the sun broke over the ridge and hit the frost on the pines, turning everything to gold and diamond, he just stood there. "This," he said, "is why we stay." Thirty years later, I understand.
— Submitted by Sarah M.

The Mercy of the Mountains

Coming Soon Your Town
I dwelt among the mountains, as I always had, yet at twenty-eight I awoke as from a long and troubled sleep. The years had passed in a haze of spirits and powders that dulled the edges of existence, leaving the soul adrift in its own dim waters. The world beyond my windows continued its eternal round, indifferent and serene, while I wandered the confines of habit, seeking oblivion where none could truly be found. One dawn, I stepped forth into the open air and paused. The vastness of the hills enveloped me, their quiet majesty unbroken by human clamor. Light filtered through the leaves, and the breeze carried the subtle voices of the wild. In that moment, something stirred within, a recognition long buried beneath layers of forgetfulness. I began to walk the paths that wound through wood and meadow, not in flight, but in return. Day upon day, I surrendered to the rhythm of the earth. The mountains rose in timeless strength, their forms a testament to endurance beyond the fleeting pains of mortality. I sat in solitude upon the heights, observing the slow passage of clouds, the play of shadow and sun across the valleys. Here was no haste, no artificial solace; only the pure unfolding of life in its essential simplicity. The cravings that had once commanded me met this greater harmony and gradually subsided, like echoes fading into silence. Through the turning of seasons, unseen hands wrought their gentle transformation. The spirit, long estranged, found itself drawn into communion with a presence vast and intimate, woven through every blade of grass and breath of wind. This was no distant deity of doctrine, but the living essence that sustains all things, revealing itself in the quiet persistence of nature. Tears came unbidden, not of despair, but of profound thankfulness for the mercy that had guided me back to wholeness. Now I live in deliberate awareness, attuned to the divine simplicity that surrounds me. The old chains have fallen away, dissolved in the clear light of these enduring hills. Purpose emerges clear and humble: to tread lightly upon the earth, to behold its wonders with open heart, to dwell in gratitude for the renewal granted me. Clean in body and spirit, I abide here, one with the transcendent order that the mountains so faithfully proclaim.
— Michael H.
